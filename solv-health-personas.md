# Solv Health User Personas

## Persona 1: Sarah Chen (Patient)

### Demographics
- **Age:** 34
- **Location:** San Francisco Bay Area, CA
- **Occupation:** Marketing Manager at a tech company
- **Income:** $95,000/year
- **Education:** Bachelor's degree
- **Family Status:** Married, 2 young children (ages 3 and 6)
- **Tech Savviness:** High - regularly uses apps for convenience

### Background
Sarah works full-time at a fast-paced tech company and juggles work commitments with parenting responsibilities. She values efficiency and convenience in all aspects of her life, including healthcare. She has moderate health insurance through her employer.

### Healthcare Needs & Behaviors
- Needs urgent care for herself and her children (ear infections, minor injuries, flu symptoms)
- Cannot afford to take full days off work for non-emergency medical issues
- Prefers same-day appointments over waiting days for primary care
- Wants price transparency before committing to a visit
- Values knowing wait times to plan her schedule
- Prefers digital check-in to avoid paperwork with sick kids in tow

### Goals
- Find convenient, same-day healthcare near home or work
- Minimize time spent waiting in crowded waiting rooms
- Understand costs upfront to avoid surprise bills
- Book appointments quickly on mobile during work breaks
- Access quality care for her family without extensive planning

### Pain Points
- Primary care physician has 2-3 week wait times for appointments
- Doesn't know which urgent care centers are reputable
- Has wasted hours at walk-in clinics with unpredictable wait times
- Surprised by bills weeks after visits
- Filling out redundant paperwork while managing sick children
- Uncertainty about whether insurance covers specific facilities

### Technology Usage
- Smartphone: iPhone (uses apps daily)
- Preferred channels: Mobile app, text notifications
- Uses Uber, DoorDash, and other on-demand services regularly
- Manages family calendar digitally
- Comfortable with telemedicine

### Quote
"I don't have time to wait around all day. I just need to know when I can be seen, how much it'll cost, and that my kids will get quality care."

## Persona 2: Dr. Michael Rodriguez (Urgent Care Owner/Operator)

### Demographics
- **Age:** 47
- **Location:** Austin, TX
- **Occupation:** Owner/Medical Director of 3 urgent care locations
- **Experience:** 15 years as physician, 8 years running urgent care business
- **Education:** MD, MBA
- **Business Size:** 3 locations, 45 total staff members

### Background
Dr. Rodriguez transitioned from emergency medicine to opening his own urgent care practice. He's responsible for both clinical quality and business operations. His clinics compete with other urgent care chains and hospital-affiliated facilities in the Austin metro area.

### Business Needs & Behaviors
- Needs to maximize patient volume while maintaining quality care
- Must manage staff scheduling efficiently across multiple locations
- Wants to reduce no-shows and fill appointment gaps
- Needs to compete with larger urgent care chains
- Requires seamless integration with existing EHR (athenaOne)
- Aims to reduce administrative burden on front desk staff

### Goals
- Increase patient acquisition and retention
- Improve operational efficiency and reduce costs
- Maintain high patient satisfaction scores
- Better predict and manage patient flow throughout the day
- Grow revenue per location by 15-20% annually
- Build brand reputation in competitive market

### Pain Points
- Front desk staff spend excessive time on phone scheduling/rescheduling
- High no-show rates (15-20%) create revenue gaps
- Difficulty attracting new patients amid competition
- Morning voicemail backlog requires 1-2 hours of staff time daily
- Limited visibility into real-time capacity across locations
- Patient acquisition costs through traditional marketing are high
- Duplicate data entry between Solv and EHR systems
- Unpredictable patient volume makes staffing challenging

### Technology Usage
- Uses EHR (athenaOne) daily for clinical documentation
- Monitors business analytics dashboards weekly
- Prefers desktop for administrative work, mobile for communication
- Values systems that integrate rather than add complexity
- Needs staff to adopt new tools easily (limited tech training time)

### Success Metrics
- Patient volume and revenue per location
- Patient satisfaction scores (NPS, online reviews)
- Staff efficiency (time spent on administrative tasks)
- No-show rates and appointment fill rates
- Marketing ROI and cost per patient acquisition
- Wait times and operational throughput

### Quote
"I need technology that helps us see more patients without burning out my staff. If it doesn't integrate with our EHR or requires extensive training, it's not worth it."

## Persona 3: Jennifer Martinez (Urgent Care Front Desk Coordinator)

### Demographics
- **Age:** 29
- **Location:** Phoenix, AZ
- **Occupation:** Front desk coordinator at urgent care clinic
- **Experience:** 4 years in healthcare administration
- **Education:** Associate degree in Healthcare Administration
- **Work Environment:** Busy urgent care clinic seeing 60-80 patients/day

### Background
Jennifer is the primary point of contact for patients at her urgent care facility. She manages phone calls, walk-ins, appointments, and patient check-in. She works closely with nurses and physicians to keep patient flow smooth.

### Daily Responsibilities
- Answer 30-50 phone calls per day for appointment scheduling
- Check-in walk-in patients and scheduled appointments
- Process patient registration and insurance verification
- Manage patient queue and communicate wait times
- Handle rescheduling and appointment confirmations
- Process payments and explain billing
- Coordinate with clinical staff on patient flow

### Goals
- Provide excellent patient service despite high volume
- Keep wait times reasonable and communicated clearly
- Reduce time spent on repetitive administrative tasks
- Minimize chaos during busy periods (mornings, post-work hours)
- Go home on time without lingering administrative work
- Maintain accuracy in patient records and scheduling

### Pain Points
- Morning voicemail backlog (20-30 messages) delays opening tasks
- Phone rings constantly, interrupting patient check-ins
- Patients get frustrated when told of long wait times upon arrival
- Manually entering patient information is time-consuming and error-prone
- Patients often arrive at wrong times or forget appointments
- Difficult to manage walk-ins vs. scheduled patients fairly
- No-shows create schedule gaps but can't fill them quickly
- After-hours patients call during busy daytime hours

### Technology Usage
- Uses EHR and scheduling system throughout the day
- Comfortable with desktop software, less so with mobile
- Appreciates intuitive interfaces (minimal training time)
- Values automation that reduces repetitive tasks
- Needs systems that are fast and reliable during peak hours

### Quote
"If patients could book themselves online and complete paperwork before arriving, I'd have so much more time to actually help people instead of being glued to the phone."

## How Solv Health Serves These Personas

### For Sarah (Patient)
- Same-day booking through mobile app
- Price transparency before appointment
- Real-time wait time visibility
- Digital check-in and pre-registration
- Quality ratings and reviews of providers
- Location-based search for convenient care

### For Dr. Rodriguez (Owner/Operator)
- AI-powered scheduling to maximize patient volume
- EHR integration to eliminate duplicate data entry
- Patient acquisition through Solv's marketplace
- Analytics on patient flow and business performance
- Reduced no-shows through automated reminders
- Lower cost per patient acquisition vs. traditional marketing

### For Jennifer (Front Desk Staff)
- Online self-service booking reduces phone volume
- Digital check-in reduces paperwork and data entry
- Automated appointment reminders reduce no-shows
- After-hours booking captures patients outside business hours
- Queue management tools for walk-ins vs. appointments
- Saves 10+ hours per week on scheduling tasks
